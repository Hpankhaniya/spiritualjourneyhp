8 Rules Of Yoga

1. Yama - Acha vyavhaar (jese ke satya bolna, non-violence, chor na karna)
2. Niyama - Acha swabhav (jese ke swachhta, santosh, tapasya)
3. Asana - Sharir ke posture (yoga poses)
4. Pranayama - Saans lene ki vidhi (breathing techniques)
5. Pratyahara - Indriyon ka niyantran (withdrawal of senses)
6. Dharana - Ek jagah man ko lagaana (concentration)
7. Dhyana - Dhyan ya meditation
8. Samadhi - Antim stage, anant shanti aur bliss

Surya Namaskar

Surya Namaskar, also known as Sun Salutation, is a traditional sequence of
twelve yoga postures that are performed in a flow. This practice is a
vital part of yoga and is known for its physical, mental, and spiritual
benefits. Each posture is synchronized with breath, creating a dynamic
sequence that enhances flexibility, strength, and balance. Surya Namaskar
helps in detoxifying the body by increasing blood circulation and stimulating
the digestive, respiratory, and nervous systems. It is also a form of worship
to the Sun God, symbolizing the divine light and energy that sustains life.
Practicing Surya Namaskar daily can lead to improved overall health, increased
mindfulness, and a deep sense of inner peace.
